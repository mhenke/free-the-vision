## Overall Impression

This is a strong campaign page with genuine personality. The Free Willy metaphor is well-executed, the copy is sharp and developer-native, and the amber-teal palette is committed. The biggest missed opportunity is the body text contrast — the `--muted` color on white is too light and undercuts the otherwise confident design. Fix that and tighten a few interaction details, and this is ready to ship.

## Priority Issues

- **[P1] Body text contrast fails WCAG AA.** `--muted` (oklch 0.35 0.008 250) on white produces approximately 2.8:1 contrast for 16px body text. WCAG AA requires 4.5:1. This affects every paragraph in the Story, State, Help, and Footer sections. Users with low vision will struggle to read the core narrative.
  - **Fix**: Darken `--muted` to at least oklch(0.30 0.008 250) for ~4.5:1, or better, oklch(0.25 0.008 250) for ~6:1.
  - **Suggested command**: `$impeccable polish`

- **[P1] Impact section text on gradient fails contrast.** The hero/impact gradient background (amber L≈0.82) with ink text (L≈0.10) produces approximately 3.6:1 — passes for large text (≥18px bold) but fails for the `.impact__label` and `.impact__source` body-size text.
  - **Fix**: Use white text on the gradient, or add a dark overlay band behind the text content.
  - **Suggested command**: `$impeccable polish`

- **[P2] Numbered steps echo the 01/02/03 scaffolding pattern.** The "How You Can Help" section uses large Bungee numbers (1, 2, 3) as section markers. While only three items, this is a known AI-slop scaffold. The numbers add visual weight but no information the headings don't already carry.
  - **Fix**: Remove the large numbers or replace with icons that add meaning (an upvote arrow, a share icon, a team icon). Alternatively, let the headings carry the hierarchy alone.
  - **Suggested command**: `$impeccable quieter`

- **[P2] Share buttons lack visible focus styles.** `.btn--share` has no `:focus-visible` rule. Keyboard users navigating the "Spread the Word" section will see no indication of which button is focused.
  - **Fix**: Add `outline: 3px solid var(--accent); outline-offset: 2px;` to `.btn--share:focus-visible`.
  - **Suggested command**: `$impeccable polish`

- **[P3] Timeline horizontal scroll is undiscoverable.** The horizontal-scroll timeline in "The State of Things" uses `overflow-x: auto` with a mask fade, but there's no visual affordance (arrow, "scroll →" hint) indicating more content is available. Users on desktop may not realize they can scroll.
  - **Fix**: Add a subtle scroll indicator or switch to a vertical layout on desktop with horizontal scroll reserved for mobile only.
  - **Suggested command**: `$impeccable adapt`

## Minor Observations

- The hero whale SVG has `opacity: 0.35` — it's subtle enough to miss on high-DPI screens. Consider bumping to 0.45 for better visibility.
- The back-to-top button appears after scrolling past the hero, which is good. Its transition is properly disabled under `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- The `onerror="this.style.display='none'"` on images is a pragmatic choice but means broken images leave no visual trace. A subtle fallback (background color, icon) would be more informative.
- The `scroll-behavior: smooth` on `html` is properly reverted under `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`.
