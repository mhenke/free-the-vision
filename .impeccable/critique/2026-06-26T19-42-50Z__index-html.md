## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment:** No AI slop detected. Avoids cream background, gradient CTAs, numbered section markers, identical card grids, frosted glass, generic typography. Bungee + Sora pairing is distinctive. Amber/teal palette commits without hedging. Section shapes vary intentionally. Copy is developer-native.

**Deterministic scan:** 1 finding flagged — `gradient-text` (false positive; hero has gradient background, not gradient text). `theater-slop-phrase` also flagged (false positive; no "theater" text exists).

**Visual overlays:** Browser injection succeeded. Console confirmed 2 false positives. No reliable overlay issues.

## Overall Impression

Well-crafted campaign page with genuine personality. The Free Willy metaphor lands with warmth, typography is bold and distinctive, palette commits. Biggest missed opportunity: mobile experience — nav vanishes on small screens with no alternative.

## What's Working

1. **Typography pairing.** Bungee + Sora delivers campaign-poster energy while staying readable. Scale is fluid and well-stepped.
2. **Section variety.** No two sections look the same. Each has its own visual world while staying on voice.
3. **Campaign copy.** "Vision is the last feature still in the tank." Developer-native, direct, lands the metaphor.

## Priority Issues

**[P1] No mobile navigation**
- Nav links `display: none` below 640px with no hamburger or alternative.
- Page will be shared on social media, mostly on mobile. Half the audience can't navigate.
- Fix: Add hamburger toggle or compact sticky nav with dropdown.
- Command: `$impeccable adapt`

**[P2] No image error handling**
- Two Unsplash images have no `onerror` fallback or CSS background fallback.
- Story section is emotional core. Broken images kill credibility.
- Fix: Add `background: var(--surface)` fallback on `.story__image`.
- Command: `$impeccable harden`

**[P2] Limited share platform coverage**
- Only Twitter and LinkedIn. No Mastodon, Bluesky, Reddit, HN.
- Campaign success depends on virality. Missing platforms where developers share.
- Fix: Add Bluesky and HN. Use `navigator.share()` as mobile fallback.
- Command: `$impeccable harden`

**[P3] Impact numbers are underwhelming**
- "8 voices" and "12 reactions" are weak social proof for a campaign page.
- Fix: Reframe or remove specific counts. Lean into "450+ days."
- Command: `$impeccable clarify`

**[P3] No back-to-top affordance**
- Long scroll page with no quick return to top nav.
- Fix: Floating button after hero, or "Back to top" in footer.
- Command: `$impeccable adapt`

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan (First-Timer):** Free Willy metaphor requires cultural knowledge. Users under 30 or outside US may not get it.

**Casey (Distracted Mobile):** Critical — no mobile navigation. Share buttons are borderline tap targets.

**Riley (Stress Tester):** Image load failures unhandled. Count-up has no JS fallback.

## Minor Observations

- `section[id]:focus { outline: none }` suppresses focus outlines; `focus-visible` follow-up is correct but programmatic `.focus()` gets no indicator.
- Whale silhouette at 25% opacity is very subtle; consider 35-40%.
- Demand section opening quote mark may clip on narrow viewports.

## Questions to Consider

1. Should impact numbers be reframed? "8 voices" reads as weak social proof.
2. Is the Free Willy metaphor self-explaining for all audiences?
3. What's the mobile priority — hamburger, bottom bar, or hero CTA sufficient?
